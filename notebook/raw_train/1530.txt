Colorado collected more than $2 million in taxes from recreational marijuana in January, its first month of sales, an accounting report released Monday showed. Medical marijuana sales added an additional $1.5 million to state coffers, according to BuzzFeed.com.Colorado residents voted for an aggressive 25% tax on recreational marijuana in November 2013. Revenue also comes from a standard sales tax as well as license application fees.Of the total revenue for the fiscal year, $40 million will go to public school construction. State lawmakers are currently debating what to do with the rest of the money, BuzzFeed.com reports.
