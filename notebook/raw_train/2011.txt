Banana distributors Fyffes and Chiquita are looking for annual supply chain savings of some 24m ($40m) from the merger of the two businesses.

The merger is expected to take place before the end of 2014 and will create ChiquitaFyffes, a business with sales of some 2.8 billion ($4.6bn).

Efficiencies in logistics and procurement are top of the list for the new organisation which expects to achieve 24m annual savings by 2016.

Chiquita is based in Charlotte, North Carolina, USA. It has sales of $3bn a year and its brands include Chiquita and Fresh Express.

Dublin-based Fyffes has an annual turnover in excess of $1.5 billion. It has operations in Europe, the US Central and South America and has begun operations in Asia.
