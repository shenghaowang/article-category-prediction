Despite a rough winter that's made a dent in many retailers' sales, Dick's Sporting Goods Inc. fourth-quarter net income rose 7 percent and margins exceeded the company's expectations.

The retailer, based in western Pennsylvania, said on Tuesday that profits rose to $138.6 million, or $1.11 a share, from $129.7 million, or $1.03 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 8 percent to $1.95 billion from $1.81 billion a year earlier for a three-month period that ended Feb. 1. The year-ago quarter included an extra week of sales.
