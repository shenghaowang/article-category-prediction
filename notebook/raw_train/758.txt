Gold for April delivery surrendered 1 percent to close at $1,338.20 an ounce on the Comex division of the New York Mercantile Exchange.

For the week, prices held onto a gain of around 1.3 percent, based on the most-active contracts, according to FactSet data.

The U.S. added 175,000 jobs in February, surpassing the 140,000 positions expected by MarketWatch. The unemployment rate rose to 6.7 percent from 6.6 percent, edging up because more people entered the labor force in search of jobs but not all of them found one.

In other metals, May silver slumped 3 percent to end at $20.93 an ounce. Prices have lost about 1.5 percent week to date.

High-grade copper for May delivery gave up 4.2 percent to $3.08 a pound, down 3.3 percent from the week-ago close.

April platinum lost 0.2 percent to $1,483.60 an ounce, ending around 2.5 percent higher for the week, while March palladium rose 0.1 percent to $781.80 an ounce, up roughly 5 percent for the week.

In energy, oil futures closed higher today, fueled by better-than-expected monthly data on nonfarm payrolls.

Following seven-consecutive weeks of gains, however, prices finished the week roughly unchanged as concerns over the situation in Ukraine calmed.

April oil gained 1 percent to finish at $102.58 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Prices settled at $102.59 last Friday.
