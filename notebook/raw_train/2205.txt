Avis, Lyft partner to make Avis cars available for Lyft drivers

Shares of Avis Budget Group Inc. rallied 2.1% in premarket trade Monday, after the car rental company announced a partnership with ride-hailing company Lyft. As part of the partnership, Avis will add thousands of vehicles to Lyft's Express Drive program, making it easier for people to drive for Lyft without owning a car. Lyft drivers will be able to reserve an Avis rental car from the Lyft app. The vehicles are expected to become available in the next few months. "With a global fleet of approximately 600,000 vehicles, we are well positioned to take advantage of the continued growth in the ride hailing space by providing on-demand access to a clean, safe and well-maintained vehicle for those who want to become ride hailing drivers," said Joe Ferraro, president of the Americas at Avis. The stock has shed 19% year to date through Friday, while car-rental rival Hertz Global Holdings Inc. shares hve slumped 28% and the S&P 500 has gained 6.2%.
