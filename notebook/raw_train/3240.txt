By: Eamonn Sheridan



Adam had the announcement covered on the weekend: China to widen yuan trading band China's yuan will be able to trade up to 2% on either side of the daily peg Up until now, the People's Bank of China had allowed trading 1% in either direction of the central reference rate Full flexibility of the yuan remains a long way off The PBOC said in its Saturday statement that it would accelerate its efforts to free trading further, but warned it would still make "necessary adjustments" if needed to keep the exchange rate stable Lu Ting, an economist at Bank of America said reform efforts will continue: "A much more important and meaningful reform is to change the rule on setting the daily fixing."
