September Unemployment Numbers: Lower in Nine States, Higher in Four

Unemployment rates in September were significantly lower than the previous month in nine states, higher in four (Colorado, Maine, North Dakota, Wyoming), and were generally stable for the rest of the 37 states and the District of Columbia, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Compared to a year ago, 37 states grew nonfarm payrolls, with the largest percentage gains occurring in Florida (4.8 percent), Utah (3.6 percent) and Texas (3.3 percent).

State unemployment rates for October 2018 will be released on Friday, November 16, 2018.

Source: U.S. Dept. of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)
