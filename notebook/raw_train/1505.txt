China's industrial production and retail sales grew less than expected in the first two months of 2014, data showed Thursday.

Industrial production increased 8.6 percent in January to February period from last year, the National Bureau of Statistics said. Production was forecast to grow 9.5 percent, following a 9.7 percent rise in December.

Another report from the statistical office showed that retail sales grew by a double-digit 11.8 percent year-on-year in January to February, but below the 13.5 percent increase forecast by economists.

Urban fixed asset investment during January to February increased 17.9 percent from the prior year, which was also slower than the expected growth of 19.4 percent.
