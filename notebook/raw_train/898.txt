China's industrial added value expanded 8.6 percent year on year in the first two months of this year, official figures revealed on Thursday.

 

On a month-to-month basis, industrial added value in February edged up slightly by 0.61 percent from the previous month, the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) said.

 

China uses industrial added value to measure business activities of designated large enterprises each with an annual turnover of at least 20 million yuan (3.26 million U.S. dollars).
