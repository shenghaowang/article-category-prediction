Symantec Corp. SYMC, +4.11% shares dropped in the extended session Thursday after the cybersecurity company's outlook fell short of Wall Street estimates. Symantec shares fell 9% after hours, following a 2.2% rise to close the regular session at $20.88. Symantec estimates adjusted earnings of 31 cents to 35 cents a share on revenue of $1.13 billion to $1.16 billion for the fiscal second quarter, and $1.47 to $1.57 a share on revenue of $4.67 billion to $4.79 billion for the year. Analysts surveyed by FactSet expect earnings of 37 cents a share on revenue of $1.19 billion for the second quarter, and $1.56 a share on revenue of $4.83 billion for the year. The company reported a fiscal first-quarter loss of $63 million, or 10 cents a share, compared with a loss of $133 million, or 22 cents a share, in the year-ago period. Adjusted earnings were 34 cents a share. Revenue fell to $1.16 billion from $1.18 billion in the year-ago period. Analysts had estimated 33 cents a share on revenue of $1.15 billion.
