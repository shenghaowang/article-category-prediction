NEW YORK (TheStreet) -- Shares of American Eagle Outfitters (AEO) are down by 4.98% to $17.36 at the start of trading on Wednesday, despite reporting 2015 second quarter earnings that beat analysts' expectations.

The company posted earnings of 17 cents per share on revenue of $797 million for the most recent quarter, beating analysts' estimates for both revenue and earnings per share.

Analysts surveyed by Thomson Reuters had forecast that American Eagle Outfitters would report earnings of 14 cents per share on revenue of $769.6 million.

Last year the company reported earnings of 3 cents per share on revenue of $710.6 million for the quarter ended August 2, 2014.

"Both American Eagle and Aerie delivered strong sales and earnings growth across channels," interim CEO Jay Schottenstein said in a statement. "We have two of the best-positioned brands in the marketplace today, and we are well poised to capitalize on our strengths."

American Eagle is an apparel and accessories retailer based in Pittsburgh, Penn. The company owns the American Eagle Outfitters clothing brand and Aerie intimates brand.
