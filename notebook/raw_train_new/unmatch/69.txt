Economic growth hit a near standstill in the first three months of this year, suffering the weakest period of activity in more than five years, according to the first official reading today.

In a performance described as ¡°undeniably worrying¡± by economists, Britain suffered a sharp fall in the construction sector, weak manufacturing growth and a slowdown in consumer spending. Together it meant that the economy grew by just 0.1 per cent in the first quarter: the weakest rate of growth since the end of 2012.

This was significantly worse than the Bank of England had been expecting and is likely to kill off the chance of a rise in interest rates when policymakers at the Bank of England meet next month.
