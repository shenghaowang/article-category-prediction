Shanghai Chaori Solar, a small Chinese solar cell manufacturer, became the first Chinese company to default on an onshore corporate bond last week, triggering a panic about further defaults among credit-strained industrial companies. Copper prices dropped by $267.50 per tonne on Friday to close the day at $6,782.50 in the wake of the bond default, giving the red metal its biggest one-day drop since December 2011.
