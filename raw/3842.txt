<p>About 30 minutes into trade, the Dow Jones Industrial Average declined 47.92 (0.29 percent) to 16,404.80.</p>
<p>The broad-based S&amp;P 500 dipped 2.09 (0.11 percent) to 1,875.95, while the tech-rich Nasdaq Composite Index edged 0.37 (0.01 percent) lower to 4,335.85.</p>
<p>Weekend economic data from China raised questions about the outlook for the world's second largest economy.</p>
<p>Beijing reported weak inflation data and a $23.0 billion trade deficit in February, while analysts had forecast an $11.9 billion surplus. </p>
<p>Meanwhile Japan said its economy grew more slowly in the fourth quarter, 0.2 percent, than the previously reported 0.3 percent.</p>
<p>Investors are also skittish over tensions between Russia and the West after Russian President Vladimir Putin insisted that Crimea had the right to join his country.</p>
<p>McDonald's became the latest big company to cite cold weather as a factor in diminished sales, saying frigid temperatures in the US were a factor in a 1.4 percent drop in comparable restaurant sales in February. Shares of the Dow component dipped 0.4 percent.</p>
<p>Boeing fell 1.9 percent after Japan Airlines Sunday said its Dreamliner jet made an emergency landing in Honolulu reportedly due to a possible problem with its hydraulic system.</p>
<p>The news followed a Friday Boeing disclosure that hairline cracks were found in the wings of some of its 787 Dreamliners that are still in production, due to a manufacturing problem.</p>
<p>Chiquita Brands International announced it was combining with Irish company Fyffes into a global produce distributor with a combined equity value of about $1.1 billion. Chiquita shareholders will own about 50.7 percent of the new company. Chiquita jumped 12.6 percent.</p>
<p>Bond prices were mixed. The yield on the 10-year US Treasury held steady at 2.79 percent, the same level as Friday, while the 30-year edged higher to 3.73 percent from 3.72 percent. Bond prices and yields move inversely.</p>
