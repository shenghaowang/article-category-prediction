<p>Japan has ruled that bitcoin is not legal tender but could still be the subject of taxation and anti money-laundering rules.</p>
<p>The Japanese Ministry of Finance admitted it did not really understand the crypto-currency despite the fact the country was home to its largest trading exchange, Mt.Gox, until it went bust last week. “It’s not money,” said finance minister Taro Aso adding that “opinions are divided” as to which agency controls it. </p>
<p>A six-page document drawn up for the government suggests that bitcoin could be treated like a commodity such as gold.</p>
