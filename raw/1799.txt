<p> <strong></strong>Asian markets slumped today, after <strong>China</strong> reported over the weekend <a class="icon none" href="http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702304732804579426001879597542?mod=article_inline">weaker-than-expected February exports data</a>. February exports slumped 18% from a year ago, surprising the market which had expected a 5% increase.</p>
<p>In addition,<strong> Beijing</strong><a class="icon none" href="http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702304020104579430192264794828?mod=article_inline">weakened its daily reference rate against the dollar by the largest since 2012</a>, in another attempt to break the one-way bet of <strong>yuan</strong> and halt the hot flow of offshore money into China. The offshore yuan dropped to 6.13 per dollar from 6.10 on Friday.</p>
<p>Asia equities slumped today on renewed concerns over a China slowdown. The<strong> Hong Kong Hang Seng Index</strong> was down 1.7%, the <strong>Shanghai Composite Stock Market Index</strong> slumped 2.9%.<strong> Taiwan</strong> and <strong>South Korea</strong> followed China lower as well. The<strong> Taiwan TAIEX Index</strong> lost 0.6%. The <strong>KOSPI Composite Index</strong> retreated 1%.</p>
<p>Commodities also suffered today. <a class="icon none" href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-03-10/copper-tumbles-to-8-month-low-in-london-on-china-demand.html?mod=article_inline" target="_blank">Copper tumbled to eight-month low</a> in London.<a class="icon none" href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-03-10/brent-crude-slides-as-china-exports-drop-wti-slips-in-new-york.html?mod=article_inline" target="_blank"> Oil fell</a> too.</p>
<p> <strong>China:</strong> </p>
<p>To boost its flailing e-commerce, <strong>Tencent Holdings</strong> (0700.HK) <a class="icon none" href="http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702304020104579429940012950168?mod=article_inline">agreed to buy a 15% stake in e-commerce site JD.com</a>. JD.com <a class="icon none" href="http://blogs.barrons.com/emergingmarketsdaily/2014/02/04/jd-com-key-takeaways-from-ipo-filing-worth-31-44-billion/?mod=article_inline" target="_blank">in January filed for an IPO in the US</a>, hoping to raise $1.5 billion. Tencent will pay $215 million in cash and transfer its e-commerce businesses to JD.com. Shares of Tencent  fell 2.2%.</p>
<p> <strong>Shanghai Chaori Solar Energy Science &amp; Technology</strong>, the first Chinese company that defaulted on a corporate bond onshore, said it planned to <a class="icon none" href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-03-10/chaori-to-sell-solar-farms-to-repay-bondholders-after-default.html?mod=article_inline" target="_blank">sell solar farms in Europe and the US to repay</a> its debt holders.</p>
<p> <a class="icon none" href="http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702304020104579430401295907692?mod=article_inline">Auto sales were up 10.7% in the first two months of the year</a>.</p>
<p> <strong>Malaysia:</strong> </p>
<p> <strong>Malaysian Airline</strong> (3786.KL) <a class="icon none" href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-03-10/malaysian-airline-retreats-most-since-2012-after-flight-vanishes.html?mod=article_inline" target="_blank">fell to a record low</a> after its<strong> Flight 370</strong> to <strong>Beijing</strong> disappeared over the weekend. The <strong>FTSE Bursa Malaysia KLCI Index</strong> was down 0.6%.</p>
<p> <strong>India:</strong> </p>
<p>Shares of <strong>Ranbaxy Laboratories</strong> (RANBAXY.BO) fell 1.5% in Mumbai after a second recall of its generic version of<strong> Pfizer</strong>'s (PFE) Lipitor drug. Ranbaxy is a unit of Japan's <strong>Daiichi Sankyo</strong> (4568-TKS), whose shares fell 1.6% today. The <strong>S&amp;P BSE 100 Index</strong> gained 0.3%.</p>
<p> <strong>Russia:</strong> </p>
<p>Russian forces tightened control over Ukraine over the weekend, paying no heed to Western sanctions. <a class="icon none" href="http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/0/8cc41172-a780-11e3-9c7d-00144feab7de.html?siteedition=intl#axzz2vYvwFRkV&amp;mod=article_inline" target="_blank">Some Western policy makers have acknowledged there is nothing they can do to force Russia to withdraw</a>. The <strong>MICEX Index</strong> is little changed.</p>
<p> <strong>Brazil:</strong> </p>
<p>Economists surveyed by the central bank <a class="icon none" href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-03-10/brazil-economists-lower-selic-forecast-for-second-straight-week.html?mod=article_inline" target="_blank">cut their 2014 growth forecast to 1.68%</a> this week, down from 1.7% last week. Economists now expect Brazil is towards the end of the tightening cycle.</p>
<p> <strong>South Africa:</strong> </p>
<p>The ruling <strong>African National Congress</strong> is <a class="icon none" href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-03-10/anc-in-talks-on-south-african-minimum-wage-to-distribute-wealth.html?mod=article_inline" target="_blank">in talks with its labor allies to impose a minimum wage </a>to help diffuse labor tensions in South Africa. The<strong> FTSE JSE 40 Index</strong> was down 0.8% this morning.</p>
