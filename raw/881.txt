<p>J.C. Penney Co., an American comeback story?</p>
<p>Yes, said Citigroup analyst Oliver Chen, who upgraded the stock to buy from neutral on Tuesday and raised his price target to $11 from $7.50. The move sent Penney shares up as much as 10.2% in early trading.</p>
<p>Chen said the company may be successful in getting back the shoppers lost during former CEO Ron Johnson’s failed reign. Penney has brought back its key private-label products including St. John’s Bay, Worthington and Ambrielle taken out under Johnson as Chief Executive Mike Ullman has said he aims to increase store brands, which are more profitable than national brands, to a level of about 50% of sales from 30% under Johnson. Meanwhile, a better assortment of national labels including Levi’s, Nike and Carter’s also are helping to bring customers back, the analyst noted.</p>

<p>Meanwhile, the company’s home merchandise, the <a href="http://blogs.marketwatch.com/behindthestorefront/2013/07/17/j-c-penneys-home-goods-upgrade-johnsons-calling-card-ullmans-albatross/">worst performin</a>g section under Johnson that had declined to 12% of sales in 2012 from nearly 20% in 2008, may also get a boost as Penney overhauls the segment. The analyst said merchandising changes in the <a href="http://blogs.marketwatch.com/behindthestorefront/2013/09/27/j-c-penney-looks-to-kids-to-ignite-sales-while-share-price-plunges/">kids’ department </a>also will be a boost.</p>
<p>Penney “isn’t taking substantial fashion risk but just going back to what worked before and marketing to drive prudent traffic,” Chen said, adding investors’ fears on the company’s liquidity position “are overdone.” He said he sees the company to end the year with $2 billion liquidity, in line with company projection. “Achieving (first quarter) and (fiscal year) guidance will mitigate investor fear on liquidity concerns and drive profitability and the stock higher.”</p>
