<p><b>Stock market update:</b></p>
<p>
</p>
<p>Stocks closed sharply lower, retreating from
last week's records as investors paused to assess recent U.S. economic data
while shrugging off uncertainties surrounding Ukraine. The Dow Jones Industrial
Average slipped 67.43 points to 16,351.25, the S&amp;P 500 index eased 9.54
points to 1,867.63 and the Nasdaq Composite Index fell 27.26 points to
4,307.19. (<a href="http://www.businessspectator.com.au/news/2014/3/12/financial-markets/wall-street-closes-lower">Business
Spectator</a>)</p>
<p>The euro fell against the dollar and the yen today
after European Central Bank policymakers indicated traders may have overlooked
the message the bank may still act to stimulate the euro zone economy. (<a href="http://www.reuters.com/article/2014/03/11/us-markets-forex-idUSBREA2211320140311">Reuters</a>)</p>
<p>In afternoon New York trade, May copper
changed hands at just under $2.96 a pound, the lowest level for the most active
futures contract since July 2010. (<a href="http://www.mining.com/copper-price-crashes-through-3-a-pound-98565/">Mining.com</a>)</p>
<p><b>Business news:</b></p>
<p>Men's Wearhouse said it would acquire
rival Jos. A. Bank Clothiers for about $1.8 billion, ending a five-month saga
that started with Jos. A. Bank offering to buy its larger menswear rival. (<a href="http://in.reuters.com/article/2014/03/11/josabank-offer-menswearhouse-idINL3N0M846E20140311">Reuters</a>)</p>
<p>
<p>Congressional efforts to shut down
bailed-out Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac took a significant step forward today as
key senators announced bipartisan agreement on a plan to overhaul the housing
finance system. (<a href="http://www.latimes.com/business/money/la-fi-mo-fannie-mae-freddie-mac-senate-housing-finance-reform-20140311,0,2529598.story#axzz2vaUqSyxn">Los
Angeles Times</a>)</p>
<p>The New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission
voted today to ban the direct sale of automobiles in New Jersey, forcing
electric-car maker Tesla to use dealers instead. (<a href="http://www.cnbc.com/id/101481668">CNBC News</a>)</p>
