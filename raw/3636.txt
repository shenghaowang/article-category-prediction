<p>Shares  of both <strong>The Men's Wearhouse Inc.</strong> <strong>(NYSE: </strong><a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/mw/" target="_blank"><strong>MW</strong></a><strong>)</strong> and <strong>Jos. A. Bank Clothiers Inc. (Nasdaq: </strong><a href="https://www.google.com/finance?q=NASDAQ%3AJOSB&amp;ei=JEQfU5jEOvSg6wGgrgE" rel="nofollow" target="_blank"><strong>JOSB</strong></a><strong>)</strong> reached  all-time highs today (Tuesday) following news that Men's Wearhouse will acquire  Jos. A. Bank for $1.8 billion. </p>
<p>MW  stock traded as high as $58.80 today, while JOSB stock touched $64.63. </p>
<p><img align="left" alt="NYSE: MW" height="198" src="http://moneymorning.com/files/2014/03/NYSE-MW.jpg" style="padding:10px" title="Jos. A Bank MW" width="265">MW offered $65  per share for JOSB. That's up from Monday's closing price of $61.83.</img></p>
<p>The  deal comes five months after Jos. A. Bank initially made an unsolicited $2.4  billion bid to buy Men's Wearhouse in October. At the time, Men's Wearhouse  rejected the offer and countered with a $1.5 billion ($55 per share) bid for  Jos. A. Bank.</p>
<p>The  two retailers have been going back and forth since. MW upped its offer twice  since the initial dealings and had most recently offered $63.50 per share. That  was once again rejected by JOSB officials, who said they would accept a higher  offer. </p>
<p>In  January, Men's Wearhouse tried to put pressure on Jos. A. Bank by nominating  two candidates to JOSB's board of directors. At the time, JOSB still wouldn't  budge. </p>
<p>The  combined companies will operate more than 1,700 stores in the United States and  employ 23,000 people. The two brands will continue to operate separately  following the deal.</p>
<p>Officials  project sales to reach $3.5 billion for the retailer. </p>
<p>Jos.  A. Bank had an $825 million deal to acquire retailer Eddie Bauer in the works,  but is terminating that purchase after today's deal. The company is also axing  a $300 million stock buy-back offer. </p>
<p>Now  that the deal has finally been made official, investors are wondering how to  play MW stock moving forward…</p>
<p>While  they're both known as retailers of men's suits and professional wear, the  acquisition actually creates the fourth-largest men's apparel retailer in the United  States.</p>
<p>After  the announcement, shares of MW stock were up 6% and shares of JOSB were up  nearly 4%. </p>
<p>Investors  who purchased JOSB stock back in October when the M&amp;A talks surfaced are  patting themselves on the back today. The offer price of $65 is a 51% premium from  the stock's price in October. </p>
<p>While  MW shareholders are pleased with the 6% bump they've enjoyed today, the future  is a little cloudier, as  some volatility can be expected in the short term. </p>
