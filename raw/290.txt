<p>Bank of England Governor Mark Carney is quizzed by MPs over claims some of the bank's officials knew about alleged foreign exchange rate fixing.</p>
<p>His appearance before the Treasury Select Committee comes a week after it emerged that some officials were aware of rigging attempts as early as 2006.</p>
<p>The bank said there was no evidence its staff had colluded to rig the market.</p>
<p>MPs are also questioning Mr Carney over other financial issues, including the possibility of a currency union.</p>
