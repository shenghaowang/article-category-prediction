<p>Mercedes-Benz could introduce a three-cylinder hybrid powertrain for its smaller vehicles, such as the GLA crossover.</p>
<p><p><a href="https://driving.ca/mercedes-benz/" target="_blank">Mercedes-Benz</a> is looking to further improve fuel economy and reduce emissions by possibly introducing a three-cylinder engine into its cars.</p><p><a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/shrinkage-mercedes-benz-considers-three-bangers-for-future-hybrids/" target="_blank">Speaking to <em>Car and Driver</em></a> at the Geneva Motor Show last week, Mercedes powertrain chief Bernard Heil believes three-cylinder engines are doable in certain vehicles thanks to increased space and better packaging if they’re mounted transversely.</p><p>However, Heil says he is not a fan of three-cylinder engines on their own due to their increased noise, vibration and harshness levels. Rather than balancing shafts and introducing a handful of other measures which could drive up costs, Heil believes the best option would be to pair the the three-cylinder engine to an electric motor.</p><p>At this point, it’s unclear whether a three-cylinder hybrid powertrain would appear in smaller vehicles, such as the B-Class, CLA-Class and GLA-Class, but Hiel maintained we won’t see a larger Mercedes with a three-cylinder in the future.</p> </p>

<p>However, Heil says he is not a fan of three-cylinder engines on their own due to their increased noise, vibration and harshness levels. Rather than balancing shafts and introducing a handful of other measures which could drive up costs, Heil believes the best option would be to pair the the three-cylinder engine to an electric motor.</p>
<p>At this point, it’s unclear whether a three-cylinder hybrid powertrain would appear in smaller vehicles, such as the B-Class, CLA-Class and GLA-Class, but Hiel maintained we won’t see a larger Mercedes with a three-cylinder in the future.</p>
