<p> Colorado reaped more in taxes from cannabis than from alcohol in January after it became the first place in ­America to allow people to smoke pot purely for fun. </p>
<p> The state raised $2.1 million (£1.2 million) in taxes from recreational marijuana, and a further $1.4 million in taxes from sales of medicinal pot, which has been permitted since 2000. The ­total of $3.5 million easily exceeded the $2.7 million in alcohol taxes that the state garnered in January a year ago, the most recent comparable figure. </p>
