<p>
	Following the market opening Tuesday, the Dow traded up 0.41 percent to 16,314.29 while the NASDAQ surged 0.51 percent to 4,301.92. The S&amp;P also rose, gaining 0.40 percent to 1,866.23.</p>
<p>
<b>Leading and Lagging Sectors</b><br/>
	In trading on Tuesday, healthcare shares were relative leaders, up on the day by about 0.67 percent. Among the leading sector stocks, gains came from Flamel Technologies SA (NASDAQ: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/flml#NASDAQ">FLML</a>), Pernix Therapeutics Holdings (NASDAQ: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/ptx#NASDAQ">PTX</a>) and Thoratec (NASDAQ: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/thor#NASDAQ">THOR</a>). Basic materials sector was the leading decliner in the US market today. Top decliners in the sector included James Hardie Industries plc (NYSE: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/jhx#NYSE">JHX</a>), off 3.2 percent, and McEwen Mining (NYSE: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/mux#NYSE">MUX</a>), down around 3 percent.</p>
<p>
<b>Top Headline</b><br/>
	Hertz Global Holdings (NYSE: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/htz#NYSE">HTZ</a>) reported <a href="http://www.benzinga.com/news/earnings/14/03/4398289/update-hertz-global-posts-downbeat-q4-earnings-plans-to-spin-off-equipme"> downbeat fourth-quarter earnings </a> and announced its plans to spin off its equipment rental business into a separate company. Hertz posted a quarterly loss of $600,000, or $0.00 per share, versus a year-ago loss of $36.8 million, or $0.09 per share. Excluding special items, it earned $0.26 per share. Its revenue surged 10% to $2.56 billion versus $2.32 billion. However, analysts were projecting earnings of $0.32 per share on revenue of $2.61 billion. Hertz expects 2014 earnings of $1.70 to $2 per share, on revenue of $11.4 billion to $11.7 billion. However, analysts expected earnings of $2.07 per share on revenue of $11.6 billion.</p>
<p>
<b>Equities Trading UP</b><br/>
	FutureFuel (NYSE: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/ff#NYSE">FF</a>) shares shot up 19.36 percent to $20.96 after the company reported a rise in its fourth-quarter earnings. FutureFuel posted its quarterly profit of $0.61 per share, versus a year-ago profit of $0.15 per share.</p>
<p>
	Shares of Yandex NV (NASDAQ: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/yndx#NASDAQ">YNDX</a>) got a boost, shooting up 4.95 percent to $31.50 after the company reported that it has purchased Israeli startup KitLocate.</p>
<p>
	FactSet Research Systems (NYSE: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/fds#NYSE">FDS</a>) was also up, gaining 8.20 percent to $113.11 after the company reported quarterly results. FactSet Research reported its Q2 earnings of $1.22 per share, versus analysts' estimates of $1.20 per share.</p>
<p>
<b>Equities Trading DOWN</b><br/>
	Shares of GameStop (NYSE: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/gme#NYSE">GME</a>) were down 4.65 percent to $37.90 as Walmart (NYSE: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/wmt#NYSE">WMT</a>) announced its <a href="http://www.benzinga.com/news/14/03/4397770/walmart-to-accept-video-game-trade-ins"> plans to begin accepting </a> video game trade-ins.</p>
<p>
	YY (NASDAQ: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/yy#NASDAQ">YY</a>) shares tumbled 2.18 percent to $79.71 after the company announced a <a href="http://www.benzinga.com/news/14/03/4396399/yy-inc-announces-proposed-offering-of-us-400m-convertible-senior-notes"> proposed offering </a> of $400 million convertible senior notes.</p>
<p>
	Shutterfly (NASDAQ: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/sfly#NASDAQ">SFLY</a>) was down, falling 5.91 percent to $47.45 after Cowen &amp; Company downgraded the stock from Outperform to Underperform and cut the price target from $57.00 to $39.00.</p>
<p>
<b>Commodities</b><br/>
	In commodity news, <a href="http://www.benzinga.com/news/14/03/4397574/bargain-hunters-boost-brent-prices"> oil traded up 0.23 percent </a> to $98.31, while gold traded down 1.52 percent to $1,352.10.</p>
<p>

<b>Eurozone</b><br/>
