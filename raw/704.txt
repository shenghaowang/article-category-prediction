The 144-year-old banana company is set to merge with its European rival, Fyffes, in a deal that would create the world’s biggest banana supplier. <span id="more-10155919"></span></p>
<p class="wp-caption-text">(<a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/armydre2008/2922375737/" target="_blank">frankieleon</a>)<br/></p>
<p>Charlotte, North Carolina-based fruit company Chaiquita Brands announced Monday it is merging with Ireland-based Fyffes in an all-stock deal valued at $1.1 billion, <a href="http://in.reuters.com/article/2014/03/10/fyffes-chiquita-brands-idINL3N0M724Y20140310" target="_blank">Reuters reports</a>. </p>
<p>The new company, which will be named ChiquitaFyffes, will control nearly 14% of the banana supplier market well ahead of Fresh Del Monte and Dole Food Company. </p>
<p>“This is a milestone transaction for Chiquita and Fyffes that brings together the best of both companies,” Ed Lonergan, Chaquita chief executive says. </p>
<p>Officials with the new company say the merger will bring new opportunities to the industry. </p>
<p>ChiquitaFyffes is estimated to have $4.6 billion in annual revenue from sales of more than 160 million boxes of bananas. </p>
<p>With approximately 32,000 employees, the new company will be based in Ireland where it will receive operational pre-tax savings of at least $40 million by the end of 2016. </p>
<p>The merger will be subject to review by competition authorities, but company officials say it is unlikely to face opposition as the two companies operate in different markets. </p>
