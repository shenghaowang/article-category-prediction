<p class="" data-reactid="272">Americans are riding the rails and buses in greater numbers than anytime since the beginning of the suburban sprawl in the 1950s.</p>
<p class="" data-reactid="273">A <a href="http://www.apta.com/resources/statistics/Documents/2013-q4-ridership-APTA.pdf" target="_blank">study</a> by the American Public Transportation Association reports nearly 10.7 billion trips were taken last year, which is the highest number of trips since 1956.</p>
<p class="" data-reactid="274">The uptick is not confined to cities like New York and Washington, D.C., which have average high populations of ridership. Cities including Seattle, Denver and San Diego have also seen a huge gain as they expand their public transportation offerings.</p>
<p class="" data-reactid="275"><strong>Cardiff Garcia</strong> of The Financial Times joins <em><a href="http://hereandnow.wbur.org">Here &amp; Now'</a>s</em> Jeremy Hobson to discuss the increasing use of public transportation.</p>
