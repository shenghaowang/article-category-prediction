<p>Gold was trading near its highest level in six months on Thursday as investors sought to hedge their bets against geopolitical tensions in Ukraine and economic slowdown fears in China.</p>
<p><strong>FUNDAMENTALS</strong></p>
<p>Spot gold was trading flat at USD 1,365.10 an ounce by 0017 GMT, after jumping 1.3 percent on Wednesday. The metal reached USD 1,370.60 in the previous session - its highest since September 19.</p>

<p>The EU agreed on a framework on Wednesday for its first sanctions on Russia since the Cold War, a stronger response to the Ukraine crisis than many expected and a mark of solidarity with Washington in the drive to make Moscow pay for seizing Crimea.</p>
<p>Markets are eyeing more economic data from China on Thursday to determine the state of the economy, after a recent bond default and a weak exports report sent equities and base metals lower. Gold is seen as a safe-haven asset.</p>
<p>SPDR Gold Trust, the world's largest gold-backed exchange-traded fund, said its holdings fell 1.50 tonnes to 811.20 tonnes on Wednesday.</p>
