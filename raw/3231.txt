<p>A train fatally struck a Metro-North employee early Monday morning in East Harlem, according to the MTA.</p>
<p>The worker was struck at Park Avenue and 106th St. by a Poughkeepsie-bound Hudson line train that left Grand Central Terminal at 12:47 a.m., <a href="http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Metro-North-Worker-Hit-By-Train-East-Harlem-249254501.html" target="_blank">NBC New York</a>  reported. He was taken to Mount Sinai Hospital, where he was pronounced dead.</p>
<p>The worker's identity is being withheld until his family is notified, the MTA said.</p>
<p>The incident is still under investigation.</p>
<p>The MTA suspended service for the emergency, and about 50 passengers were transferred to another train, and their trip resumed around 2:15 a.m. Service was restored by the morning.</p>
