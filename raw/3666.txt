<p class="Meta-content"><time class="Dateline">March 11 2014, 12:01am, </time><span class="Publication">The Times</span></p>
<p> Business should not get “hung up” about when interest rates will start to rise and instead take confidence in the fact that they will increase slowly to a level far lower than they were before the crisis, a deputy governor of the Bank of England has argued. </p>
<p> Charlie Bean told business leaders in Darlington yesterday that the Bank would do its “utmost to ensure the recovery is not nipped in the bud”, warning that although the economy was “on the mend”, risks remained. </p>
<p>Growth cannot continue to be sustained by consumer spending and housing investment, he said. “We really want to see three things happen: business investment needs to pick up . . . a revival in productivity growth . . . and an expansion in net exports.”…</p>
