<p>Your daily look at late-breaking news, upcoming events and the stories that will be talked about Wednesday:</p>
<p><b>1. CIA ACCUSED OF MISCONDUCT</b></p>
<p>A powerful senator accuses the agency of interfering with a congressional investigation into the CIA's possible use of torture during Bush-era terror probes.</p>
<p><b>2. MALAYSIAN PLANE HUNDREDS OF MILES OFF COURSE WHEN IT VANISHED</b></p>
<p><a href="http://www.nola.com/news/index.ssf/2014/03/malaysia_airlines_flew_hundred.html">Military radar data adds confusion and mystery</a> to one of the most puzzling aviation incidents of recent years.</p>
<p><b>3. GOP PREVAILS IN TEST OF 'OBAMACARE'</b></p>
<p>Republican David Jolly, who campaigned on a pledge to repeal the health care law, wins a hard-fought congressional race in Florida.</p>
<p><b>4. WASHINGTON STATE COUPLE DUMPED INTO GARBAGE TRUCK</b></p>
<p>A couple who were sleeping in a dumpster with their dog <a href="http://www.krem.com/news/local/Man-woman-dog-sleeping-in-dumpster-tossed-into-Spokane-garbage-truck-249442671.html#">were awakened Tuesday morning when they were thrown into the back of a garbage truck</a> in Spokane. When the driver heard a lot a noise, he pulled over and called for help. Firefighters arrived and found the man, woman and dog inside the truck. Medics took them to a hospital as a precaution, but they didn't appear to have any injuries other than bruising.<br/><b><br/>5. GM FACING CRIMINAL PROBE</b></p>
<p>The Justice Department is examining why GM waited until February to recall 1.6 million compact cars after knowing about a problem with ignition switches for a decade.</p>
<p><b>6. WHO'S ARGUING THAT AMERICAN CHEESE SHOULD BE JUST THAT</b></p>
<p>As part of trade talks, the European Union wants to ban the use of European names like Parmesan, feta and Gorgonzola on cheese made in the U.S.</p>
<p><b>7. TV PREACHER VICTIM OF THEFT</b></p>
<p><a href="http://www.nola.com/crime/index.ssf/2014/03/600000_theft_reported_from_saf.html">Thieves break into a safe at Joel Osteen's megachurch in Houston</a> and make off with $600,000 in cash and checks.</p>
<p><b>8. WORLD'S OLDEST KNOWN MASKS ON DISPLAY IN JERUSALEM</b></p>
<p>The 11 stone masks date back 9,000 years and offer a rare glimpse at some of civilization's first communal rituals.<br/><b><br/>9. WHERE OBAMA USED HIS CREDIT CARD</b></p>
<p>Visiting Manhattan, <a href="http://www.nola.com/news/index.ssf/2014/03/obama_shops_at_the_gap_guess_w.html">he goes shopping at the Gap</a>, buying sweaters for his daughters and an athletic jacket for his wife.<br/><b><br/>10. BYE, BIG D</b></p>
<p>Franchise sack leader DeMarcus Ware is released by the Dallas Cowboys as the NFL's free-agency signing period opens. <div><br/></div><div><br/></div><div><br/></div></p>
