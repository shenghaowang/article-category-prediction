<p><a href="http://etfdailynews.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/data.bmp" rel="magnific"><img alt="data" class="alignleft wp-image-16670" height="110" src="http://etfdailynews.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/data.bmp" style="border: 1px solid black;" width="110"/></a><a href="http://advisorperspectives.com/dshort/" target="_blank">Doug Short:</a> The University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment preliminary number for March came in at 79.9, a decline from the 81.6 February final. Today’s reading was below the <a href="http://www.investing.com/economic-calendar/?eventid=35472#35472" target="_blank">Investing.com</a> forecast of 82.0. The index is off its 85.1 interim high set in July of last year.</p>
<p>See the chart below for a long-term perspective on this widely watched indicator. I’ve highlighted recessions and included real GDP to help evaluate the correlation between the Michigan Consumer Sentiment Index and the broader economy.</p>
<p><a href="http://advisorperspectives.com/dshort/charts/indicators/Sentiment.html?Michigan-consumer-sentiment-index.gif" rel="magnific" target="_blank"><img alt="Click to View" border="1" height="467" src="http://advisorperspectives.com/dshort/charts/indicators/Michigan-consumer-sentiment-index.gif" style="border: 1px solid black;" title="Click to View" width="595"/></a></p>
<p><a href="http://advisorperspectives.com/dshort/charts/indicators/Sentiment.html?Michigan-consumer-sentiment-index.gif" rel="magnific" target="_blank">Click for a larger image</a></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">To put today’s report into the larger historical context since its beginning in 1978, consumer sentiment is now 6 percent below the average reading (arithmetic mean) and 5 percent below the geometric mean. The current index level is at the 34th percentile of the 435 monthly data points in this series.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The Michigan average since its inception is 85.1. During non-recessionary years the average is 87.5. The average during the five recessions is 69.3. So the latest sentiment number puts us 10.6 points above the average recession mindset and 7.6 points below the non-recession average.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">It’s important to understand that this indicator is somewhat volatile with a 3.1 point absolute average monthly change. For a visual sense of the volatility here is a chart with the monthly data and a three-month moving average.</p>
