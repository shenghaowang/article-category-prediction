<p>
  U.S.  stocks closed mixed today, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average incurring  losses, while the S&amp;P 500 and the tech-heavy Nasdaq noted modest gains.</p>
<p>
<a href="http://www.moneymorning.com/tag/dow-jones-industrial-average" target="_blank"><strong>The  Dow Jones Industrial Average</strong></a><strong> </strong>closed down 0.07% or 11 points,  to 16,340; <a href="https://www.google.com/finance?q=INDEXSP%3A.INX&amp;ei=k5jmUsmxO6GK6gHj5QE" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">the S&amp;P 500</a> closed up 0.03%, or 0.5 points,  at 1,868 points; and <a href="https://www.google.com/finance?q=INDEXNASDAQ%3A.IXIC&amp;sq=nasdaq&amp;sp=1&amp;ei=vJjmUoD2LMyy6wHR8AE" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">the Nasdaq</a> closed up 0.5%, or 16 points, at  4,323 points.</p>
<p>
  Energy  futures fell today, with crude oil, heating oil, and natural gas futures  significantly off. Light, sweet crude oil for April delivery is down 2.1% at $97.91  per barrel today; heating oil is down 1.4% at $2.92 per gallon; and natural gas  is down 1.7% at $4.52 per million BTUs.</p>
<p>
  Meanwhile,  metals futures rose, with gold trading up 1.5% at $1,367.50 per ounce, platinum  trading up 0.7% at $1,475.30 per troy ounce, and silver rising 2.2% at $21.28  per ounce.</p>
<p>  Facebook  Inc. (Nasdaq: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/fb/" target="_blank">FB</a>) shares traded up about 1% today  as the company claimed headlines for two separate stories. </p>
<p>
  First,  Facebook's Chief Operating Officer Sheryl Sandberg is rumored to be the  possible next head of The Walt Disney Co. (NYSE: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/dis/" target="_blank">DIS</a>), according to coverage that  started with the New York Post. Sandberg is on Disney's board of directors.  However, other reports are skeptical of the speculation, which is unattributed.</p>
<p>
  Second,  Facebook's headquarters in Menlo Park, Calif., were on lock down yesterday  until late in the evening due to a "possible threat."</p>
<p>

<p>  Several  stocks bucked today's down market on positive news, including SolarCity Corp. (Nasdaq: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/scty/" target="_blank">SCTY</a>) and BG Medicine Inc. (Nasdaq: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/bgmd/" target="_blank">BGMD</a>). </p>
<p>
  BG  Medicine shares were up as much as 20% today after the life sciences company  said late Tuesday that it received approval to continue listing on Nasdaq after  proving compliance.</p>
<p>
  BG Medicine  likely also received a boost after La Jolla Pharmaceutical welcomed positive  results from a phase 2 clinical trial on chronic kidney disease. The study met  primary endpoints using BG Medicine's BGM Galectin-3 test.</p>
<p>
  And  Solar City Corp. (Nasdaq: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/scty/" target="_blank">SCTY</a>) shares rose more than 5% after  the solar company partnered with Best Buy Inc. (NYSE: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/bby/" target="_blank">BBY</a>) to launch solar-panel  consulting and sales locations in about 60 of Best Buy's store.</p>
<p>  Herbalife  Ltd. (NYSE: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/hlf/" target="_blank">HLF</a>) and Express Inc. (NYSE: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/expr/" target="_blank">EXPR</a>) were among the stocks incurring  losses on top of the overall stock market slump.</p>
<p>
  Herbalife  shares plunged about 9% after trading was halted as it said it received a Civil  Investigative Demand from the Federal Trade Commission after volatile trading.  The nutrition company said it will cooperate in the probe and said it is in  compliance with FTC regulations.</p>
<p>
  And, Express  shares plummeted more than 12%, marking a 52-week low of $15.51 in today's  session on its fourth-quarter results, with both earnings and revenue falling  below the Street view.</p>
<p>
  Express  reported $0.57 per share in earnings and $715.9 million in revenue versus the  Street view of $0.59 per share and $722 million in revenue.</p>
