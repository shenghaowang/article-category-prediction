<p itemprop="articleBody"><span class="xn-location" itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Place">LOS ANGELES</span>, <span class="xn-chron">March 11, 2014</span> /Emag.co.uk/ — Verde Media Group Inc. (OTC: VMGI)</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">As reported by <span class="xn-person" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">John Ingold</span> of the Denver Post.  <span class="xn-location" itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Place">Colorado</span> collected slightly more than <span class="xn-money">$2 million</span> in taxes on the sale of recreational marijuana in the month of January, according to new figures released by the state Monday.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">The numbers are the first official tallies of the historic January sales, themselves a nationwide first.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">In January, Colorado <a href="https://www.colorado.gov/revenue" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">collected $1.4 million</a> from a special 10 percent sales tax on recreational marijuana, plus another <span class="xn-money">$416,690</span> from the state’s standard 2.9 percent sales tax, according to a news release Monday from the Colorado Department of Revenue. Extrapolating from those figures, recreational marijuana businesses did slightly more than <span class="xn-money">$14 million</span> in retail sales during the month.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">Most of the tax revenue came from stores in <span class="xn-location" itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Place">Denver</span>, and the city reported it collected <span class="xn-money">$616,282</span> in local sales taxes from recreational marijuana sales in January. In addition, the state will share <span class="xn-money">$128,586</span> with the city from statewide collections.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">Recreational marijuana businesses paid <span class="xn-money">$195,318</span> in excise tax during January, which will be put toward school construction.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">In total, 59 recreational marijuana businesses filed returns in January.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">Both Gov. <span class="xn-person" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">John Hickenlooper’s</span> budget office and legislative analysts have predicted recreational sales for the first six months of 2014 would top $190 million.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody"><span class="xn-person" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">William Veve</span>, President of Verde Media Group, Inc. stated, “Again the business landscape in the legal marijuana market is validated by significant collection of tax revenue.”</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody"><strong class="c5">About Verde Media Group Inc.</strong></p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">Verde Media Group Inc. is a publicly traded company listed on the OTC Markets trading under the stock symbol: <strong>VMGI</strong>. Verde Media Group Inc.  The company is consists of three divisions:</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody"><span class="c6">Agency Division</span>–  The innovative Agency division services- public relations, marketing, and transaction functions for corporate clientele.  The agency is rapidly expanding its business in the <span class="xn-location" itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Place">Denver, Colorado</span> market that will become the largest retail marijuana sector currently legitimized. </p>
<p itemprop="articleBody"><span class="c6">BioTech Division</span>– The VMBD division undertakes the business of developing and producing high value bio-products from renewable biomass.  The focus and criterion is to develop proprietary microbial technologies with low risk scale-up methods employing low cost feedstock.  VMBD will further develop the derivative and ester forms of THC for increase potency and their use in different delivery systems, including sublingual, inhaled dosage forms and blending with food products.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody"><span class="c6">Entertainment Division</span>–  The company operates a managed media company with production, distribution, development, and acquisition functions for film and television.</p>
