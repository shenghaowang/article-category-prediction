<p><a href="https://moneymorning.com/2014/02/18/king-ipo-tastes-bad-investors/" target="_blank"><em>King IPO</em></a><em><strong>:</strong></em> King  Digital Entertainment PLC said today (Wednesday) it expects a market value of as  much as $7.6 billion in its initial public offering.</p>
<p><img align="left" alt="King IPO" src="http://cdn.moneymorning.com/files/2014/03/20140312_king-ipo.jpg" style="padding:5px;" title="King IPO" width="300"/></p>
<p>On Feb. 18, the maker of several hit social Internet games, including tour  de force "<a href="https://moneymorning.com/tag/candy-crush-saga/" target="_blank">Candy Crush  Saga</a>," filed for a U.S. initial public offering (<a href="https://moneymorning.com/tag/ipo-2014/" target="_blank">IPO</a>). The  stock will be listed on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol  "KING," with J.P. Morgan Chase &amp; Co. (NYSE: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/jpm/" target="_blank">JPM</a>), <a href="https://moneymorning.com/tag/credit" target="_blank">Credit</a> Suisse Group AG (NYSE: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/cs/" target="_blank">CS</a>), and Bank of America Merrill Lynch (NYSE: <a href="https://moneymorning.com/stocks/bac/" target="_blank">BAC</a>) serving as lead underwriters.</p>
<p>Today,  King said its IPO of 22.2 million shares will likely be priced between $21 and  $24 per share. </p>
<p>The  company itself will sell 15.5 million shares in the offering, while  stockholders, such as Apax Ventures, will sell 6.7 million shares. According to  the IPO filing, entities related to Apax will own  44.2% of the company following the offering. Founder and Chief Executive  Officer Riccardo Zacconi will maintain a 9.5% stake.</p>
<p>The  King IPO's official pricing is set for later this month, on March 25. All in  all, it's projected to raise as much as $532.8 million – a smidge over the $500  million figure King had disclosed in the February filing.</p>
