<p>The following are the M&amp;A deals, rumors and chatter circulating on Wall Street for Tuesday March 11, 2014:</p>
<p><b>Men's Wearhouse to Acquire Jos A Bank for $65.00/Share</b></p>
<p><i>The Deal</i>:<br/> The Men's Wearhouse (NYSE: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/mw#NYSE">MW</a>) and Jos. A. Bank (NASDAQ: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/josb#NASDAQ">JOSB</a>) announced Tuesday that Men's Wearhouse will acquire all of the outstanding shares of common stock of Jos. A. Bank for $65.00 per share in cash, for total consideration of $1.8 billion.  The deal has been approved by the boards of directors of both companies. </p>
<p>On February 27, after months of resisting, Jos. A. Bank finally agreed to meet with Men's Wearhouse. On March 3, Men's Wearhouse announced a Non-Disclosure Agreement with Jos. A. Bank.</p>
<p>As a condition of the transaction, Jos. A. Bank has terminated its agreement to acquire Eddie Bauer parent Everest Holdings. The deal is expected to close by Q3 2014.</p>
<p>Men's Wearhouse closed Tuesday at $57.14, gaining almost 5%. Jos. A. Bank closed at $64.22, gaining almost 4%.</p>
<p><b>Report Jive Software Considering Sale</b></p>
<p><i>The Rumor</i>:<br/>Shares of Jive Software (NASDAQ: JIVE) rose sharply Tuesday on a report from <i>Re/code</i> (<a href="http://recode.net/2014/03/11/collaboration-software-maker-jive-explores-possible-sale/">http://recode.net/2014/03/11/collaboration-software-maker-jive-explores-possible-sale/</a>) that the company has been exploring the possibility of a sale, according to sources. The sources report Frank Quattrone's Qatalyst Partners has been hired to assist in the process of finding a buyer.</p>
<p>A spokesperson for Jive declined comment on the rumor.</p>
<p>Jive Software closed Tuesday at $8.52, a gain of 2.5%.</p>
<p><b>Elliot Management Responds to Riverbed</b></p>
<p><i>The Response</i>:<br/>Elliott Management issued a public response Tuesday to statements made by executives of Riverbed Technology (NASDAQ: <a class="ticker" href="/stock/rvbd#NASDAQ">RVBD</a>), which the activist investor labeled as "grossly misleading."  Elliott filed a presentation by Moelis &amp; Company (<a href="http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/904495/000119312514093183/d691370dex99f.htm">http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/904495/000119312514093183/d691370dex99f.htm</a>) as part of an amended 13D, which Elliott says "validates the attractiveness of Elliott's $21-per-share bid for Riverbed." </p>
<p>Elliott was responding to comments Riverbed CEO Jerry Kennelly and CFO Ernie Maddock, who told <i>Bloomberg</i> that no “serious” party had made a “credible” bid for the Company.</p>
<p>Riverbed has rejected offers of $19.00 per share and $21.00 per share from Elliott.</p>
<p>Riverbed Technology closed Tuesday at $20.06, a loss of $0.15.</p>
