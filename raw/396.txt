<p>The yellow metal shot up on Wednesday with the spot price hovering around $1,370 per ounce, a 1.5% gain, and gold's highest spot price since September.</p>
<p>Developments in China and Ukraine are likely the driving forces.</p>

<p>While China's first domestic bond default sank the copper market on Tuesday, it may have boosted gold on Wednesday as signs of unstable financial conditions pushed some investors to wealth-preserving bullion.</p>
<p>Things are also heating up again in Ukraine. A referendum on Crimea's separation is scheduled for Sunday and the country's security council chief has accused Russia of a "<a href="http://www.ctvnews.ca/world/ukraine-accuses-russia-of-military-buildup-at-its-border-1.1725205" target="_blank">massive military buildup</a>" at its borders.</p>
<p>The EU, Canada, Japan, Turkey, and the US have threatened Russia with coordinated sanctions.</p>
<p>"Risk events in China and Ukraine are supportive factors for gold because are generating some safe-haven demand," one analyst told <a href="http://www.reuters.com/article/2014/03/12/markets-precious-idUSL3N0M92EI20140312" target="_blank">Reuters</a>.</p>
