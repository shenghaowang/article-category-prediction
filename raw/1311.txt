<p>LOUISVILLE, Ky. (WDRB) -- Indiana lawmakers could soon have a say in childcare.<br/></p>
<p>A bill being negotiated in the General Assembly Monday would require all child care centers that receive taxpayer money to have nutritional and safety standards.<br/></p>
<p>Limits would also be set on how many children each center could oversee.<br/></p>
<p>These changes come after an unlicensed Indianapolis day care owner faces charges after four children were hospitalized due to Benedryl overdoses.</p>
<p>The bill needs final approval from the House, Senate and Governor before becoming law.</p>
