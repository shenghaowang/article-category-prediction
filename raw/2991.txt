<p>Top German court confirms legality of eurozone bailout fund</p>
<p class="column small-12 c-social-share__title">Share this article</p>
<p>Germany’s Constitutional Court has confirmed the eurozone’s bailout fund is legal. </p>
<p>At issue was the 700 billion euro fund known as the European Stability Mechanism, to which Germany is the biggest single contributor at 27 percent of the total. </p>
<p>The judges has been asked to rule on whether the <span class="caps">ESM</span> violated the rights of Germany’s lower house of parliament – the Bundestag – to decide on budgetary matters: </p>
<p>Andreas Vosskuhle, the President of the Federal Constitutional Court, said: “The result is clear. The constitutional complaint is partly inadmissable and apart from that without merit”</p>
<p>More than 35,000 people had complained to the court, included academics and lawmakers of all political stripes.</p>
<p>The judges rejected their argument that the <span class="caps">ESM</span> illegally transfered sovereignty from Berlin to Brussels, saying there were sufficient safeguards and limits to Germany’s contributions.</p>
<p>The court stressed that measures had been taken to ensure Germany’s liability to the <span class="caps">ESM</span> was limited to 190 billion euros, with any increase subject to approval or veto by the Bundestag.</p>
