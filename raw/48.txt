  <div class="body"><a href="http://www.alibaba.com/" target="_blank">Alibaba</a> is a privately owned Hangzhou-based group of Internet-based e-commerce businesses including business-to-business online web portals, online retail and payment services, a shopping search engine and data-centric cloud computing services. In 2012, two of Alibabaâ€™s portals together handled 1.1 trillion yuan ($170 billion) in sales, more than competitors eBay and Amazon.com combined.
<div style="padding-bottom:15px;"></div>

According to the <a href="http://online.wsj.com/news/articles/SB10001424052702303546204579440472954450720" target="_blank">Wall Street Journal</a>, the company is planning for a flotation in the U.S., saying the move will make it "a more global company".
<div style="padding-bottom:15px;"></div>

The company predicts that the listing will raise up to $15 billion. However, it has to be revealed when the initial public offering (IPO) would take place or on which exchange.
<div style="padding-bottom:15px;"></div>

The news comes two days after another Chinese tech giant, Twitter-like service Weibo, announced plans, reported by the <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-26588397" target="_blank">BBC</a>, for a $500m U.S. listing.
