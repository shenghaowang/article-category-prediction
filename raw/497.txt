<p class="p1">The American company Chiquita has struck a deal with the Irish <a href="/topics/fyffes/">Fyffes</a> to create ChiquitaFyffes, the world’s largest banana firm with an approximate worth of $1 billion.</p>
<p>“This is a milestone transaction for Chiquita and <a href="/topics/fyffes/">Fyffes</a> that brings together the best of both companies,” said Chiquita head <a href="/topics/ed-lonergan/">Ed Lonergan</a>, in <a href="/topics/bbc/">BBC</a>.</p>
<p>The newly formed company will be listed in New York, and shareholders will get shares in both firms, <a href="/topics/bbc/">BBC</a> reported. <a href="/topics/fyffes/">Fyffes</a>‘ shareholders will own just at 49.3 percent of the merged firm, <a href="/topics/bbc/">BBC</a> said.</p>
<p>The deal was made with both cost cuts and expansions in mind, <a href="/topics/ed-lonergan/">Mr. Lonergan</a> said.</p>
