<p style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13px; background-color: #ffffff; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px;">Quiznos has filed for bankruptcy protection, five days after the Sbarro LLC pizza chain did the same, <a href="http://r20.rs6.net/tn.jsp?e=001shT7DmGi6XiPCXQIdIfabm3SJWbGv5mtU37Dv48wwnf2M53dbLhHkkDQz4iiRqNnzseoXjNqGL3E6Ff-oVM8uG31dUFJNWpR9ygN9ozVu9mAozKPCKigWD36Z2JCBUdR3-VqAxrRVJwHJ82UQe_neLGeutd_xLAPlTXX22bZ7SBFP7PVXbhEq5Rg_cMSQw0RWvKJ_MCyF_ivW9JoVmbajw==" linktype="1" shape="rect" style="color: #1a63a1;" target="_blank" track="on">CNNMoney reported</a>. Executives at the restaurant chain, known for its toasted sandwiches, agreed to a restructuring plan that will reduce its debt by more than $400 million, the company said in a statement today. All but seven of the 2,100 Quiznos restaurants in the United States and 30 other countries are independently owned franchises, and will remain open and operating as usual. </p>
