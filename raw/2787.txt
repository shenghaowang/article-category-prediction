<p><strong><a href="https://www.govconwire.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Digital-Globe.jpg"><img alt="DigitalGlobe" class=" wp-image-53189 alignleft" height="162" sizes="(max-width: 163px) 100vw, 163px" src="https://www.govconwire.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Digital-Globe.jpg" srcset="https://www.govconwire.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Digital-Globe.jpg 272w, https://www.govconwire.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/Digital-Globe-150x150.jpg 150w" width="163"/></a><a href="https://www.govconwire.com/?s=DigitalGlobe" target="_blank">DigitalGlobe</a></strong> (NYSE: DGI) has launched a program with its <em>Tomnod</em> crowdsourcing platform to help in the search of the <strong>Malaysia Airlines</strong> Boeing 777 jetliner that went missing on Saturday.</p>
<p>The <em>FirstLook</em> subscription service works to compate pre- and post-event satellite imagery for emergency management functions,  the company <a href="http://www.digitalglobeblog.com/2014/03/10/missingmalayairjet/" target="_blank">said Monday</a>.</p>
<p>Two DigitalGlobe satellites have collected images of the search area,  which includes the Gulf of Thailand and the South China Sea.</p>
<p>Volunteer Tomnod users then analyze the images for clues to the possible location of the missing aircraft.</p>
<p>Acquired by DigitalGlobe in 2013,  Tomnod has previously helped response and recovery activities in the aftermath of natural and man-made disasters such as Typhoon Haiyan in 2013.</p>
