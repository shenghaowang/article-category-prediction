<p>US consumer prices rose slightly for the second month in a row in February, led by gains in food prices, the Labor Department said Tuesday.</p>
<p>The consumer price index rose 0.1 percent, matching the January gain. Food prices, rising 0.4 percent, accounted for more than half of the increase.</p>
<p>Energy prices fell 0.5 percent as a drop in gasoline prices outweighed sharp rises in fuel oil and natural gas prices.</p>
<p>Excluding food and energy prices, which tend to be volatile month-over-month, core CPI rose 0.1 percent for the third consecutive month.</p>
<p>Analysts on average had forecast a 0.2 percent rise in CPI.</p>
<p>Compared with a year ago, February consumer prices increased 1.1 percent, slowing from 1.6 percent in January. Core CPI rose 1.6 percent from February 2013.</p>
<p>The latest consumer prices data pointed to continued subdued inflationary pressures as the Federal Reserve opens a two-day monetary policy Tuesday.</p>
<p>The Fed's preferred inflation indicator, the personal consumption expenditures price index, was up 1.2 percent year-on-year in January, well below the central bank's 2.0 percent target for price stability.</p>
