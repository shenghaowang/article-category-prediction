<p>A Metro-North worker was struck and killed by a train early Monday morning while working on the tracks, according to <a href="http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2014/03/10/metro-north-worker-struck-killed-by-train/" rel="nofollow" target="new">report from CBS</a>. </p>
<p>The employee, identified as James Romansoff, 58, was taken to the hospital where he was pronounced dead. </p>
<p>About 50 passengers onboard the Poughkeepsie-bound train were placed on another train and resumed their trip, the report said. </p>
<p>Disruptions were caused for about two hours, but service has since been restored, the report said.</p>
<p>Metro-North and MTA police are investigating the incident.</p>
