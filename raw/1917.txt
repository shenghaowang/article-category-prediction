<p><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">Chinese Premier Li Keqiang on Thursday at a press conference 
in Beijing after the 
closing meeting of the second annual session of China's 12th National People's 
Congress (NPC), China's parliament, said future defaults of financial products 
in China are “unavoidable” but the government will take measures to ensure they 
do not pose a threat to the wider financial system. The premier also said that 
economic growth would be kept in a reasonable range in 2014.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">The premier said China needs to step up supervision of financial risks and he 
added that "isolated cases" of debt default were difficult to avoid.<br>
<br>
		
"We must strengthen monitoring and deal with the problems in a timely way to 
make sure there are no systemic or regional risks," he said and added that a 
national audit of local government debt last year showed that debt risks are 
controllable overall.</br></br></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">Last week China had its first domestic bond default in modern times when 
Chaori Solar, a solar panel producer, failed to pay interest on Rmb¥1bn ($162m) 
worth of bonds it sold two years ago.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">Bloomberg News reports that credit-default swaps to protect Chinese sovereign 
bonds from non-payment for five years climbed to 95.8 basis points or 0.958%  
yesterday, the highest since Feb. 5, according to prices from data provider CMA.
</span>
</p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">“We could see more defaults, not limited to solar companies but also in 
industries with excess capacity like steel and mining,” Steve Wang, head of 
fixed-income research in Hong Kong at BOCI Securities Ltd told Bloomberg. "Trust 
products may also face defaults, he said.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">"We are not preoccupied with GDP growth," Li 
said, <span style="font-weight: bold;">adding that jobs and income levels as well as controlling pollution 
were important goals.</span></span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">Meanwhile, Xinhua, China's official news agency, 
reported that <span style="font-weight: bold;">China's</span> <span style="font-weight: bold;">urban fixed-asset investment</span> grew 17.9% year on year in the first 
two months of 2014, down from the 21.2% growth registered in the same 
period of last year, official data showed today.</span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">The growth rate was 1.7%age points lower than the full-year pace of 2013, 
as China's economy still faces downward pressure, the National Bureau of 
Statistics (NBS) announced on Thursday.</span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">Urban fixed-asset investment amounted to Rmb¥3.03tn (US$494.13bn) during January and February, with the growth pace in February up 1.33%age points on a month-to-month basis.</span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-weight: bold; font-size: 10pt;">China's retail sales grew 11.8% year on year</span><span style="font-size: 10pt;"> to Rmb¥4.23tn ($690bn) in the first two months of the year, the 
NBS also said Thursday.</span></span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">The growth rate was 13.6% in December.</span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial;"><span style="font-weight: bold; font-size: 10pt;">China's industrial added value</span><span style="font-size: 10pt;"> expanded 8.6% year on year in the first 
two months of 2014, official figures revealed on Thursday.</span></span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">On a month-to-month basis, industrial added value in February edged up slightly 
by 0.61% from the previous month, the NBS added.</span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">China uses industrial added value to measure business activities of designated 
large enterprises with an annual turnover of at least Rmb¥20m ($3.26m) each.</span></p>
<p>
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 10pt;">The statistics showed that the added value of state-owned enterprises saw 4.4% growth in the two months, while joint stock companies ended the period 
with 9.9% growth.</span></p>
