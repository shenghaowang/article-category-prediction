<p>Despite a rough winter that's made a dent in many retailers' sales, Dick's Sporting Goods Inc. fourth-quarter net income rose 7 percent and margins exceeded the company's expectations.</p>
<p>The retailer, based in western Pennsylvania, said on Tuesday that profits rose to $138.6 million, or $1.11 a share, from $129.7 million, or $1.03 a share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 8 percent to $1.95 billion from $1.81 billion a year earlier for a three-month period that ended Feb. 1. The year-ago quarter included an extra week of sales.</p>
<p>"We generated strong results in our fourth quarter, with record earnings per share of $1.11, above the upper end of the guidance range we provided … as our sales and merchandise margin exceeded our expectations," said CEO Edward W. Stack in a statement.</p>
<p>On Nov. 19, Dick's said it expected earnings per share of $1.04 to 1.07.</p>
<p>In the Lehigh Valley, there are three Dick's Sports Goods locations — in Whitehall, Easton and Stroudsburg.</p>
