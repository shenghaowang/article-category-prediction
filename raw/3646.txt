<p>KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia — Malaysia Airlines believes the co-pilot aboard the missing plane spoke the last words to ground controllers. </p>
<p> Malaysia Airlines CEO Ahmad Jauhari Yahya said at a news conference Monday that initial investigations indicate that co-pilot is the one who calmly said, "All right, good night." </p>
<p class="story-continued">Story Continued Below</p>
<p> Officials previously have said that those words came at a point in the March 8 flight when one of the jetliner's data communications systems already had been switched off. </p>
<p> The timing of the last words has sharpened suspicions that one or both of the pilots may have been involved in the plane's disappearance. </p>
