<p class="headline">What are Ages for Social Security Early Retirement 
		and Full Retirement?</p>
<p class="subhead">Social Security Q&amp;A this week answers this and has 
		more information for disabled veterans on faster processing of their 
		application for help</p>
<p class="NewsStory">
<font face="Arial, Helvetica, Verdana">
<font class="content" xmlns:fo="http://www.w3.org/1999/XSL/Format">
<font face="Arial, Helvetica, Verdana" size="2">

		15, 2014  The age for applying to receive Social Security benefits used 
		to be simple, because it stayed the same. Now, the age for receiving 
		full benefits keeps moving higher. But, what about early retirement, a 
		reader asks? The Social Security Q&amp;A has the answer. It also provides 
		more details for disabled veterans on the recent move to high priority 
		for disability assistance requests.</p>
<p class="NewsStory"><b>Question:</b></p>
<p class="NewsStory"><b>My full retirement age is 66 years and 4 months 
		because I was born in 1956. What is the earliest age I can begin 
		receiving retirement benefits? Is it 62 or 62 and 4 months?</b></p>
<p class="NewsStory"><i><u><b>Answer: </b></u></i></p>
<p class="NewsStory">The earliest age you can begin receiving Social 
		Security retirement benefits is 62. Although the full retirement age is 
		increasing from age 66 to 67 for people born after 1954, you can still 
		apply as early as age 62 regardless of what your full retirement age may 
		be.  </p>

<p class="NewsStory"><b>Question:</b></p>
<p class="NewsStory"><b>I am a disabled veteran. I have a compensation 
		rating of 100% Permanent and Total from the Veterans Administration. 
		Will I automatically qualify to receive Social Security disability?</b></p>
<p class="NewsStory"><i><u><b><span lang="EN">Answer:</span></b></u></i></p>
<p class="NewsStory">No. You would have to apply for Social Security 
		disability and go through the application process.  </p>
<p class="NewsStory">You may have heard where Social Security recently 
		announced rules regarding an expedited process for processing disability 
		applications filed by veterans with a Department of Veterans Affairs 
		(VA) disability compensation rating of 100% Permanent &amp; Total (P&amp;T). </p>
<p class="NewsStory">Carolyn W. Colvin, Acting Commissioner of Social 
		Security recently unveiled a new initiative to expedite disability 
		claims.  Under the new process, Social Security will treat these 
		veterans applications as high priority and issue expedited decisions, 
		similar to the way the agency currently handles disability claims from 
		Wounded Warriors. </p>
<p class="NewsStory">Our veterans have sacrificed so much for our 
		country and it is only right that we ensure they have timely access to 
		the disability benefits they may be eligible for and deserve, said 
		Acting Commissioner Colvin.  </p>
<p class="NewsStory">Social Security worked with Veterans Affairs to 
		identify those veterans with disabilities who have a high probability of 
		also meeting our definition of disability.  I am proud of our 
		collaboration and happy to announce this new service for Americas 
		vets. </p>
<p class="NewsStory">In order to receive the expedited service, veterans 
		must tell Social Security they have a VA disability compensation rating 
		of 100% P&amp;T and show proof of their disability rating with their VA 
		Notification Letter. The VA rating only expedites Social Security 
		disability claims processing and does not guarantee an approval for 
		Social Security disability benefits.  These veterans must still meet the 
		strict eligibility requirements for a disability allowance. </p>
<p class="NewsStory">Social Security plans to launch the expedited 
		process in mid-March. For information about this service, please visit
		<a href="http://socialsecurity.gov/pgm/disability-pt.htm" style="text-decoration: none underline; text-underline: single; text-line-through: none" target="_top">
		socialsecurity.gov/pgm/disability-pt.htm</a>. </p>
<p class="NewsStory">For more about Social Securitys handling of 
		Wounded Warriors disability claims, visit
		<a href="http://socialsecurity.gov/woundedwarriors" style="text-decoration: none underline; text-underline: single; text-line-through: none" target="_top">
		socialsecurity.gov/woundedwarriors</a>. </p>
