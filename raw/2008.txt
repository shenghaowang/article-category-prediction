<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><b>TOKYO:</b> Toyota has shut down production at its two auto-assembly plants in India and locked out 6,400 workers amid testy wage negotiations.<o:p></o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify">Toyota Motor Corp. spokesman Naoki Sumino said some workers were disrupting production. There have been no injuries or damage at the plants.<o:p></o:p></p>
<p style="border:0px;margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px;padding:0px;">Â </p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify">The lockout came Monday following negotiations that began in April last year, and arbitration talks that failed earlier this year.<o:p></o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify">The two plants in Bangalore produce 310,000 vehicles a year, including the Innova van, Fortuner sport-utility vehicle, Corolla subcompact and Etios models.<o:p></o:p></p>
<p><span style="text-align: justify; line-height: 1.538em;">Toyota has been eager to expand in India with sales of its affordable models.</span></p>
