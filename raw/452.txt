<p> Teen clothing retailer American Eagle Outfitters, Inc. (<a href="http://www.rttnews.com/SymbolSearch.aspx?Symbol=AEO" runat="server" style="text-decoration:none;">AEO</a>) Tuesday reported a sharp fall in fourth-quarter profit, reflecting asset impairment charges and write-offs. Total net revenues declined 7 percent from last year, with a drop in comparable sales.</p>
<p>However, adjusted earnings as well as revenues beat analysts' expectations. The company also issued a weak outlook for the first quarter. The shares are down more than 6 percent in the early morning.</p>
<p>Looking ahead, the Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania-based company said <span class="landLinks"><a href="http://www.rttnews.com/Content/Business.aspx">business</a></span> conditions remain challenging, with severe winter weather contributing to weak demand. </p>
<p>Based on a high single-digit decline in comparable sales, it expects earnings per share for the first quarter to be about breakeven compared to adjusted earnings of $0.18 per share reported last year. This forecast excludes potential asset impairment and restructuring charges, the company said.  </p>
<p>Analysts are looking for earnings of $0.13 per share for the first quarter. </p>
<p>Jay Schottenstein, Interim CEO said, "The Company's results in 2013 were highly disappointing. While tough macro conditions have persisted in our retail sector, our merchandise and overall customer experience fell short of expectations." </p>
<p>On January 22, 2014, American Eagle named Chairman Jay Schottenstein as interim chief executive officer, after Robert Hanson surprisingly announced his resignation.</p>
<p>For the fourth quarter, the company's net income plunged to $10.51 million or $0.05 per share from $94.78 million or $0.47 per share reported last year.  </p>
<p>Earnings per share for the recent quarter included charges of $0.22 per share related to asset impairment, asset write-offs and corporate charges as well as related tax.  </p>

<p>On an adjusted basis, the firm recorded earnings of $0.27 per share for the 13-week period ended February 1, 2014. Adjusted earnings were $0.55 per share for the 14-week period ended February 2, 2013. </p>
<p>On average, 27 analysts polled by Thomson Reuters expected earnings per share of $0.26 for the quarter. Analysts' estimates typically exclude one-time items.</p>
<p>Total net revenue for the 13 weeks fell 7 percent to $1.04 billion from $1.12 billion for the 14-week period last year. Analysts estimated revenues of $1.03 billion for the quarter.</p>
<p>Consolidated comparable sales were down 7 percent, following a 4 percent comparable sales increase last year.  </p>
<p>AE Total Brand comparable sales declined 7 percent, aerie Total Brand reported a 4 percent drop, while, AEO Direct showed an 8 percent increase in comparable sales.  </p>
<p>Operating margin decreased 770 basis points to 8.2 percent.  </p>
<p>AEO is currently trading at $13.30, down $0.91 or 6.40 percent, on a volume of 2.90 million shares on the NYSE. </p>
