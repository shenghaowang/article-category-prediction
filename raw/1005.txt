<p><img alt="libyan" height="312" src="//www.brecorder.com/images/stories/july/libyan_400.jpg" style="margin-right: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px; float: left;" width="400"><span lang="EN-GB">BENGHAZI</span><span lang="EN-GB">: </span><span lang="EN-GB">Libya</span><span lang="EN-GB">‘s rebel army on Saturday said it hoped to recapture areas to the south of </span><span lang="EN-GB">Tripoli</span><span lang="EN-GB"> over the next 48 hours, as they attempt to push within striking distance of the capital.</span></img></p>
<p class="MsoPlainText"><span lang="EN-GB">After a retreat from around the plains town of Bir al-Ghanam some 80 kilometres (50 miles) from Tripoli, spokesman Colonel Ahmed Omar Bani said the rebel army would soon try to push the frontline northward.</span></p>
<p class="MsoPlainText"><span lang="EN-GB">“In the next two days the (revolutionaries) will come up with answers, things will change on the frontline,” he said.</span></p>
<p class="MsoPlainText"><span lang="EN-GB">Bir al-Ghanam is at the foot of the Nafusa Mountains, where NATO forces have been bombarding loyalist positions.</span></p>
<p class="MsoPlainText"><span lang="EN-GB">Two armoured vehicles belonging to Qadhafi forces were destroyed in the town on Friday night.</span></p>
<p class="MsoPlainText"><span lang="EN-GB">The mountains were the site of a controversial French weapons drops for the rebels and have been one of the few battle lines which have shifted in recent weeks, mostly in the rebels’ favour.</span></p>
