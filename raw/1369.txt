<p class="description">A roundup of trading on major world markets:</p>
<p><b>NEW YORK</b> - US stocks followed foreign markets lower as investors retreated in the face of concerns about Ukraine and the Chinese economy.</p>
<p>Five minutes into trade on Wednesday, the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 61.55 (0.38 per cent) to 16,289.70.</p>
<p>The broadbased S&amp;P 500 lost 8.97 (0.48 per cent) at 1,858.66, while the tech-rich Nasdaq Composite Index declined 21.90 (0.51 per cent) to 4,285.29.</p>
<p>The losses were smaller than in Europe, where equity markets in Britain, France and Germany all fell by more than one per cent.</p>
<p><b>LONDON</b> - Europe&amp;aposs main stock markets have closed down, with mining shares hit hard by renewed worries over China&amp;aposs economic outlook.</p>
<p>The London&amp;aposs FTSE 100 lost 0.97 per cent to 6,620.9 points on Wednesday, the Dax 30 in Frankfurt slumped 1.28 per cent to 9,188.69 points while Paris&amp;aposs CAC 40 ended the day down 1.0 per cent to 4,306.26 points compared with Tuesday&amp;aposs closing values. "Equity markets have come under pressure around the world as copper prices continue to fall following weak economic data from China," said Chris Beauchamp, an analyst at trading firm IG. "Although copper prices have bounced slightly today, the drop through three-year lows yesterday unnerved markets.</p>
<p>"Throw in some headlines about Russian movements in Crimea, and you have the recipe for a continuing sell-off," he added.</p>
<p>The euro gained to $US1.3910 from $US1.3863 late in New York the day before.</p>
<p><b>HONG KONG</b> - Asian markets sank, taking their lead from another sell-off on Wall Street, while Tokyo took a hit as the yen climbed against the US dollar with profit-takers moving in following last week&amp;aposs greenback rally.</p>
<p>Despite a small pick-up on Tuesday, regional markets followed Wall Street&amp;aposs cues in the wake of the weekend&amp;aposs worse-than-expected trade and inflation data from China and a downward revision to Japanese economic growth in 2013.</p>
<p>Tokyo tumbled 2.59 per cent, or 393.72 points to finish at 14,830.39 and Sydney fell 0.55 per cent, or 29.6 points, to 5,384.2 while Seoul shed 1.60 per cent, or 31.33 points, to 1,932.54. Shanghai ended down 0.17 per cent, or 3.47 points, at 1,997.69 and Hong Kong fell 1.65 per cent, or 367.66 points to 21,901.95.</p>
<p><b>WELLINGTON</b> - The NZX 50 Index fell 5.407 points, or 0.1 per cent, to 5096.530.</p>
