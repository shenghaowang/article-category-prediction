<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">New York's Metro-North Railroad says one of its employees was struck and killed by a train while working on tracks in Manhattan.</font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">The nation's second-busiest railroad says it happened at 12:54 a.m. Monday at Park Avenue and East 106th Street.</font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">The employee was taken to a hospital where he was pronounced dead. His identity wasn't released.</font></p>
<p class="imageCaption">Dead: A Metro-North employee was struck and killed by a train in Manhattan while doing work on the tracks Monday morning, MTA officials confirmed</p>
<p> </p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">About 50 passengers were aboard the train, the 12:47 a.m. from Grand Central Terminal bound for Poughkeepsie.</font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">Service was suspended while emergency crews worked, and the 50 passengers on the train that hit the worker were transferred to another train resuming their trip at 2:17 a.m.</font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">The death is being investigated by Metro-North and MTA police.</font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">Service was restored before the morning rush.</font></p>
<p class="imageCaption">Timeline: The Hudson Line train departed Grand Central Terminal at 12:47 a.m. en route to Poughkeepsie. Approximately 50 passengers on the train were safely moved to another train and resumed their trip at 2:17 a.m.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">Metro-North serves 281,000 riders a day in New York and Connecticut.</font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">It has experienced a spate of problems in the last year, including a derailment in the Bronx that killed four passengers and injured 71.</font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">Last May, a </font><font style="font-size:1.2em;">derailment in Bridgeport, Connecticut, injured 76.</font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">In the same month, track foreman Robert Luden, 52, 
was killed in Connecticut after a student rail controller put the track 
where Luden was working back into service without approval. <br/></font></p>
<p><font style="font-size:1.2em;">Last nights death comes after Metro-North's new president, Joseph Giulietti, vowed to improve safety on the 31-year-old railroad.<br/></font></p>
