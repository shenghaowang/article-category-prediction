<p>Shares of Sun Pharmaceuticals on Thursday slumped by as much as 6.5 per cent following a <a href=" http://businesstoday.intoday.in/story/usfda-bans-imports-from-sun-pharmaceutical-gujarat-plant/1/204244.html" mce_href=" http://businesstoday.intoday.in/story/usfda-bans-imports-from-sun-pharmaceutical-gujarat-plant/1/204244.html" target="_blank" title="USFDA bans imports from Sun Pharma's Gujarat plant, shares hit">ban on import of products</a> made at its Karkhadi plant in Gujarat by the US Food and Drug Administration due to violation of manufacturing norms.</p>
<p>After opening on the positive note, shares of the <a href="http://businesstoday.intoday.in/story/sun-pharmaceutical-seeks-to-enter-japan-generics-market/1/204030.html" mce_href="http://businesstoday.intoday.in/story/sun-pharmaceutical-seeks-to-enter-japan-generics-market/1/204030.html" target="_blank" title="Sun Pharma seeks to enter Japan generics market">drug firm</a> went into a tailspin following the ban and plunged 6.35 per cent to Rs 565.60 at the BSE.</p>
<p>On the NSE, the stock slipped 6.42 per cent to Rs 565.50.</p>

<p>Comments from the company could not be obtained immediately.</p>
<p>This is the first import alert for any of Sun Pharma's plants from the USFDA, which has been cracking down heavily on firms such as Ranbaxy and Wockhardt that have not complied with its manufacturing norms.<br/></p>
