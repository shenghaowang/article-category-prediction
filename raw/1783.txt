<p>UniCredit shocks with 14 billion euro balance sheet clean-up</p>
<p class="column small-12 c-social-share__title">Share this article</p>
<p>Leading Italian bank UniCredit has posted a massive loss of 14 billion euros for 2013.</p>
<p>Much of that was as it wrote off bad loans that it knows will never be repaid, along with so-called goodwill – the paper value of assets – in Italy, Austria and central and eastern Europe.</p>
<p>The writedowns are to clean up its balance sheet ahead of checks on the financial health of European lenders by the region’s regulators.</p>
<p>Without them Unicredit would have made a 916.5 million euros net profit in 2013, according to the consensus of forecasts from a poll of analysts carried out by Thomson Reuters </p>
<p>It will also cut 8,500 jobs by 2018 There will be 5,700 layoffs in Italy, 1,500 in Germany and 900 in Austria. </p>
<p>The bank sharply wrote down the value of its acquisitions since 2005 which was when it became a major force in Europe through the purchase of Germany’s HypoVereinsbank and its extensive operations in Central and Eastern Europe.</p>
<p>It will sell its Ukrainian subsidiary and has set aside 600 million euros to cover losses on that. </p>
<p>Unicredit is also planning to list its Fineco online banking unit and to sell its bad loans management unit.</p>
<p>The moves were welcomed in the financial markets which noted Italy’s biggest bank in terms of assets is not going to have to raise more capital. </p>
<p>UniCredit’s shares rose 6.04 percent.</p>
