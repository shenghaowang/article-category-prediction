<p>SINGAPORE: Gold climbed for a second session on Wednesday to its highest in 4½ months, as global uncertainty over economic growth and tensions in Ukraine burnished the metal's safe-haven appeal.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>Weakness in equities and base metals, along with technical buying, propelled gold prices through resistance at US$1,355 an ounce, traders said.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>Other precious metals were higher too, with silver climbing over 1%.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>"There is quite a lot of attention on moves in other metal markets, like base metals," said one Hong Kong-based precious metals. </p>
<p> </p>
<p>"It is possible we are seeing some move from base to precious metals. We are seeing some technically fuelled buying as well," he said.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>"With temperatures rising in the market, people are sensing that gold's downside maybe more limited than some of the other markets." </p>
<p> </p>
<p>Spot gold had gained 0.6% to US$1,357.11 an ounce by 0309 GMT, after earlier hitting US$1,359.25 – its highest since Oct 29. The metal gained 0.8% on Tuesday.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>Bullion has gained 13% this year following a 28% drop in 2013, on escalating uncertainties around the world. </p>
<p> </p>
<p>Ukraine's government appealed for Western help on Tuesday to stop Moscow annexing Crimea but the Black Sea peninsula, overrun by Russian troops, seemed fixed on a course that could formalise rule from Moscow within days. </p>
<p> </p>
<p>China's first bond default and weak data on exports have stoked concerns about the health of the world's second biggest economy. Copper and iron ore prices have been hit the hardest.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>In a sign of investor confidence in bullion, gold-backed exchange-traded products saw inflows of US$500mil in February, a reversal from 13 consecutive months of outflows,according to BlackRock.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>PHYSICAL MARKETS</p>
<p> </p>
<p>Demand for physical bullion and jewellery has dropped off due to the jump in prices.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>In China, the world's biggest gold consumer, local prices are at a discount to spot prices of about US$3 an ounce. Demand has waned since the Lunar New Year holiday, before which prices were at a premium of US$20.  </p>
<p> </p>
<p>Pakistan has extended its ban on gold imports to March 31, as it tries to stop smuggling to India where high import duties have made supplies scarce. months, as global uncertainty over economic growth and tensions in Ukraine burnished the metal's safe-haven appeal.</p>
