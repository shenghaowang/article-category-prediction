<p>Car sales rise again as European economies recover</p>
<p class="column small-12 c-social-share__title">Share this article</p>
<p>European car sales rose by 7.6 percent in February from a year ago, with the gradual economic recovery boosting demand for mass-market brands.</p>
<p>Sales grew 17.8 percent in Spain, 40.2 percent in Portugal and 20.6 percent in Ireland.</p>
<p>Italy, Europe’s fourth-biggest car market, saw sales increase by 8.6 percent .</p>
<p>But in the second-largest – France – they fell 1.4 percent. </p>
<p>Registrations in Germany, the top car market, underperformed the regional trend, with a 4.3 percent increase.</p>
<p>European car sales have suffered a six-year slump, falling to their worst in 20 years, as people cut back on expensive purchases amid austerity and uncertainty about job security.</p>
<p>Context is everything however, and industry players pointed out the gains comes from historic lows. In Italy, for example, car sales are recovering from levels last seen in the 1970s.</p>
<p>“Sales have effectively been growing, but discounting practices have been following a very similar path, it seems,” Carlos Da Silva, an analyst with market researchers <span class="caps">IHS</span> Automotive, said in a note. “This would tend to indicate that the overall situation remains more tense than it might appear.”</p>
<p><strong>Breakdown by marque</strong></p>
<p>Sales at Renault Group jumped 11.5 percent, boosted mainly by a 33.6 percent surge in registrations of its cheaper Dacia brand. </p>
<p>Ford sales were up 11.3 percent. Toyota Group, the world’s biggest-selling car maker, posted a 14 percent increase, and General Motors saw sales go up 12.3 percent, boosted by a 15.6 percent increase in registrations of its Opel- and Vauxhall-branded vehicles.</p>
<p>Germany’s Volkswagen group, Europe’s biggest carmaker by volume, posted a 7.2 percent rise, helped by a 21.5 increase at its value brand Skoda, a 15.7 percent jump in Seat sales and an 11.8 percent gain at its premium brand, Audi. VW’s performance was weighed down by a 0.8 percent decline in sales at its namesake brand.</p>
<p>French carmaker <span class="caps">PSA</span> Peugeot Citroen lost some market share in February. Its overall sales grew 3.5 percent, supported by an increase in registrations of its Peugeot brand, but sales of Citroen-branded cars were roughly flat year-on-year.</p>
