<p>LOUISVILLE, Ky. (WDRB) -- Louisville Metro Police say they&amp;aposve arrested a man after he was caught driving a U.S. Postal Service truck while intoxicated.</p>
<p>According to an arrest report, it happened March 8, on Preston Hwy., just before 6 p.m.</p>
<p>Police say they received a report of a U.S. Postal Service truck "weaving all over the roadway and not stopping at red lights."</p>
<p>They caught up with the truck and allegedly found 59-year-old Edward C. Willard, a U.S. Postal Service employee, behind the wheel. At the time, police say Willard&amp;aposs truck was in two lanes of traffic, and witnesses were standing near the truck trying to talk to Willard.</p>
<p>Willard was "very incoherent, confused and could not follow directions," according to the arrest report.</p>
<p>He then allegedly tried to drive off after stating everything was okay.</p>
<p>Police say witnesses told them they saw him drinking from a bottle of Mountain Dew. According to the arrest report, officers found water bottles inside the vehicle, but they contained vodka. </p>
<p>Willard was arrested and charged with operating a motor vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs.</p>
