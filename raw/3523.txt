<p>Chinese authorities have published images of what they suggest may be three pieces of wreckage from the missing Malaysia Airlines plane. </p>
<p>The website of China's State Administration of Science carries three satellite images taken on Sunday - a day after the plane went missing. </p>
<p>The images, which appear to show fragments in the sea, were only published on Wednesday. Co-ordinates alongside them would place the objects in the South China Sea between Malaysia and Vietnam. </p>
<p>The BBC's Sarah Campbell explains.</p>
