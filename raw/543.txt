<p>King Digital Entertainment, the largest game developer on 
  <a href="https://www.nasdaq.com/symbol/fb">Facebook</a>

  , announced terms for its IPO on Wednesday. The Dublin,
  Ireland-based company plans to raise $500 million by offering
  22.2 million shares (30% insider) at a price range of $21 to $24.
  At the midpoint of the proposed range, King Digital Entertainment
  would command a fully diluted market value of $7.4 billion. We
  have previously reported on
  <a href="http://www.renaissancecapital.com/ipohome/news/us-ipo-recap:-candy-crush-ipo-is-now-in-play-17992.html" rel="nofollow">key comparisons between King and Zynga.</a>
<br/>
<br/>

  King, which was founded in 2002 and booked $1.9 billion in sales
  for the fiscal year ended December 31, 2013, plans to list on the
  NYSE under the symbol KING. The company initially filed
  confidentially on July 16, 2013. J.P. Morgan, Credit Suisse, BofA
  Merrill Lynch, Barclays, Deutsche Bank and RBC Capital Markets
  are the joint bookrunners on the deal.</p>
