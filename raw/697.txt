<p>U.S. mortgage application activity improved lately, according to the results of the Mortgage Bankers Association’s (MBA) most recent survey. The MBA reported last week that the seasonally adjusted Market Composite Index increased 9.4% in the week ended February 28. The Refinance Index ticked up 10%, while the Purchase Index climbed 9%, in the said period. According to the survey, the share of applicants seeking to refinance their existing loans edged down to 57.7%.</p>
<p>As for national average mortgage rates, Freddie Mac reported last Thursday that the average rate on the 30-year fixed loan dipped to 4.28%. A week earlier this type of loan was carrying 4.37% in interest. With regards to the 15-year fixed loan, the average rate looked more attractive last week at 3.32%, the mortgage-finance company reported in its weekly survey.</p>
